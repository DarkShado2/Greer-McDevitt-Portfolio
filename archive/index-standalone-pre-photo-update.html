About Me

I’m an Aeronautical and Astronautical Engineering student at Purdue University with hands-on experience in bipropellant liquid rocket propulsion, high-pressure fluid systems, and rotating detonation engines. I enjoy taking projects from initial concepts through design, analysis, and integration into test-ready systems.

I thrive in fast-paced, iterative environments where I can take ownership of challenging problems, work directly with hardware, and iterate quickly. I’m especially motivated by roles that involve real engineering responsibility and close collaboration with multidisciplinary teams to continuously expand my technical skill set.

I’m actively seeking internship opportunities to continue building experience in propulsion, fluids, and aerospace systems. Let’s connect!

Education and experience

I am currently on an engineering Co-op at Shield AI, supporting propulsion and fuel system development for the X-BAT autonomous VTOL aircraft. My work focuses on the roll-control system, as well as the design of test infrastructure to characterize nozzle performance and pressure losses across COTS components.

At Purdue Space Program, I serve as the Responsible Engineer for the Phoenix qualification injector, a 700 lbf LOX-centered pintle injector developed to validate a new liquid propulsion test cell. Zucrow Laboratories, where I designed and built high-pressure fluid systems for rotating detonation engine injector testing. My responsibilities include developing panel CAD assemblies and P&IDs, fabricating GSE, and supporting RDE hot-fire test operations.
